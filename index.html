Portfolio

Estimate My App

This was my final project while at DevPoint Labs. We were asked to build an application to assist with DevPoint Studios bidding for new projects. My main contribution was developing all of the on click functions of the individual features and updating all of the subtotals and totals. This project is built with a Ruby on Rails back end, and React JS front end.

Trello Style Project Board

A trello style project board that I modeled towards a helicopter work order management system. This project was built with Ruby on Rails using html.erb for the views. Most of the database queries are custom find by SQL queries. This project taught me tons about authentication with Devise and allowing different types of users to do different things as well as a good understanding of Active Record paramaters.

Rock, Paper, Scissors

This project focused on conditional statements to determine who wins in games of Roshambo. It was also my first project modifying the DOM with JavaScript. I probably went overboard with games and rounds, but it gave me more experience by adding a level of complexity to the code. Click 'Check It Out' to go play a few rounds. I have built other versions of this game using jQuery and ReactJS.

Etch-a-Sketch

Event listeners, document query selectors, adding classes, attributes and apending/removing children were used heavily in making the Etch-a-Sketch. Ruby Shopping Cart

I initially made this project with JavaScript and rewrote it with Ruby. Even with my limited coding knowledge, it is true that once you learn one programming language, the rest come easier. You can add items to your cart, remove items, view your cart and total, and checkout; purchasing all the items and resetting the hash to being empty.

Ruby Orange Tree

This game is an Oregon Trail style game. You start with some money, some fertilizer and an orange tree. You must fertilize, water, pick oranges, sell oranges, buy fertilizer etc... to keep your tree alive and make money. Be careful not to over water or fertilize your tree or you might kill it. Pick all your oranges before each year passes to avoid losing them. This was a really fun project to work on, and can continually be expanded the more that I learn.

Google Homepage Re-creation

Google Results Re-creation

The Google search result page recreation was very similar to the home page project. There were more elements to work with making the layout of the page tricker. I have already learned much better ways to layout the search result page by building this "About Me" page.

Growing Up

Farm Yard
Truck Unloading Grain
Harvest

I grew up in rural North Dakota, 10 miles from the nearest tiny town, about five miles from the Canadian border. Most of my childhood was spent playing outside in the sandbox, building snow forts and riding with Dad in the tractor. Eventually, I would end up driving all the tractors and doing nearly all the work my dad did on the farm. I was never into sports, as my after school activities usually revolved around helping Dad.

I always remember being around a computer. We started off with DOS, and I was able to learn to navigate that pretty early in life. I remember how great Windows 3.1 was when it came out. I built my first computer my senior year in high school and was the "IT assistant" during the three free hours I negotiated myself that year as well. I've always been quite comfortable using electronic devices.

My parents told me I needed to go out and get some education, and then come back and farm if I wanted to do that. During a career/college fair I went into the auditorium because it had the comfiest chairs and that presentation was about aviation maintenance. It was only a two year college and seemed like it would be fun so I decided I would go learn how to fix airplanes. After two years of college I ended up in Utah, and never made it back to ND to farm.

Helicopters

Heavy Maintenance

My career in helicopter maintenance started in 2006 at a small company based out of the Bountiful SkyPark Airport. We performed heavy maintenance on our own and customers helicopters along with work all around the intermountain west. I spent most of my summers in Tucson, AZ or Kalispell, MT providing field support on Forest Service fire fighting contracts.

U of U AirMed

I then switched jobs to work for the largest helicopter operator in the world, Air Methods, where I worked with the University of Utah AirMed program. The helicopter I was responsible for wasbased in Tooele, UT. Day to day life as an EMS helicopter mechanic was quite boring, and required being on call24 hours a day, 5 days a week.

USFS Contract

My next employment move was to a medium size company based out of Denver. I managed to end up working on the same Forest Service contract that I was on in prior years. I was there for 3 years before Teagan was born and I decided a career change was due.
