My History

Early Life

Helicopters

Heavy Maintenance

My career in helicopter maintenance started in 2006 at a small company based out of the Bountiful SkyPark Airport. We performed heavy maintenance on our own and customers helicopters along with work all around the intermountain west. I spent most of my summers in Tucson, AZ or Kalispell, MT providing field support on Forest Service fire fighting contracts.

U of U AirMed

I then switched jobs to work for the largest helicopter operator in the world, Air Methods, where I worked with the University of Utah AirMed program. The helicopter I was responsible for wasbased in Tooele, UT. Day to day life as an EMS helicopter mechanic was quite boring, and required being on call24 hours a day, 5 days a week.

USFS Contract

My next employment move was to a medium size company based out of Denver. I managed to end up working on the same Forest Service contract that I was on in prior years. I was there for 3 years before Teagan was born and I decided a career change was due.

Skills

Google Homepage Re-creation

This is an attempt at copying the Google home page using only HTML and CSS. The Google Chrome "inspect element" feature was used heavily in this recreation. I learned about using image sprites to grab multiple sections of an image to create the logo.

Google Results Re-creation

The Google search result page recreation was very similar to the home page project. There were more elements to work with making the layout of the page tricker. I have already learned much better ways to layout the search result page by building this "About Me" page.

Rock, Paper, Scissors

This project focused on using conditional Javascript statements to decide if the computer or user wins a round of rock, paper, scissors. There are also functions to keep the running tally of computer or user wins to determine who wins the game based on the number of rounds the user inputs they want to play. The CSS is less than desireable on this, but the project initially started as only working in the console.

Etch-a-Sketch

Event listeners, document query selectors, adding classes, attributes and apending/removing children were used heavily in making the Etch-a-Sketch. Like my Rock, Paper, Scissors project, this isn't very responsive and not visually appealing, but learning Javascript was the purpose of this exercise.
